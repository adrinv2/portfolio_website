"Fall" Publication


A response piece to a play titled "VAL" performed in Het National Theatre.

The play itself was about falling,—to fall, the act of it, the emotional fall, different ways to…

It was recently unveiled that Duchamp's "Fountain" was perhaps not his but made by a female artist by the name of Baroness Elsa von Freytag-Loringhoven. This book was made to bring her out of her fall, drowned out by the tides of history

"The Waste Land" Publication

A micro-typography publication, in the module "Micro-typography" within Letter Studio, supervised and taught by Frank E. Blokland.

"The Waste Land" is a five part poem by T.S. Eliot reflecting on the horrors of the First World War, that consequently shaped a generation of authors following. This publication includes the full poem and a section of notes made by T.S. Eliot himself, included in the original copy. (**DOUBLE CHECK**)

SIX-STEPS:
"Population response to Traumatic Experiences"

A publication done under Susana Carvalho's "Six Degrees of Inspiration".

It focuses specifically on the paper "The Trajectory of Perpetrators’ Trauma: Mnemonic Politics around the Asia-Pacific War in Japan" by Kiyoteru Tsutsui. In this paper are listed the different ways in which the Japanese population responds; denial, justification, evasion, displacement, projection and universalism. These terms then shaped the layout of the content itself.
